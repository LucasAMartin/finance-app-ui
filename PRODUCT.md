# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Busy professionals who check their finances during short windows throughout the day. They need to glance at their current balance, spending trends, and budget status without friction. Context: mobile device, multiple times daily, quick decision-making mode—not deep exploration.

## Product Purpose

A mobile finance tracking app that visualizes personal spending across categories and time periods using the 50/30/20 budget framework. Success means users can spot spending patterns and budget overages at a glance, and understand their financial health via clear visual metrics. The app serves daily accountability and trend awareness for professionals managing their own money.

## Brand Personality

Premium, refined, trustworthy. Design communicates sophistication and precision through restraint—elegant charts, clear typography, intentional spacing—not decoration. Users should feel confident that their financial data is being handled with care.

## Anti-references

- **Overly gamified interfaces** (badges, points, reward notifications, achievement systems). Finance is serious; avoid game-ification that trivializes money.
- **Complex, overwhelming UX** (hidden features, nested menus, too many options per screen). Busy professionals need clarity and fast access, not exploration.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity at a glance** — Visual hierarchy should surface the most important metric (current spending, budget status) immediately; secondary data layers fade until needed.
2. **Refined minimalism** — Premium feel achieved through intentional spacing, thoughtful color restraint (sage/sky/rose palette), and high-quality typography. Do not decorate; let data speak.
3. **Data-forward design** — Charts and numbers are the interface; UI elements exist only to frame and contextualize them.
4. **Low friction** — Remove navigation layers; surface key metrics on the main screen; swipe or tap to compare periods, not dial through menus.
5. **Trustworthy precision** — Readable typography, accurate color contrast, explicit labels. Numbers should never ambiguous; data integrity is brand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific legal requirements stated. Follow WCAG AA best practices: sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for text), readable font sizes (14px minimum body), and support for system dark mode preference. Test color palette against red and blue color blindness.
